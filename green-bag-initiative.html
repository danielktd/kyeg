Executive Summary

The Green Bag Initiative-Uganda is project organised by Kikaaya Youth Empowerement Group (KYEG) aims to combat environmental degradation in Uganda by eradicating the use of plastics and polythene bags . This project envisions a cleaner, greener and more sustainable country through widespread awareness, education and the promotion of eco-friendly alternatives to plastic bags.

The Green Bag Initiative- Uganda project campaign will take atleast for 20 years in order to to pave the way to a Plastic-Free Future.

Problem Statement

In Uganda, the pervasive use of polythene bags and plastics has led to severe environmental degradation, posing a substantial threat to the country's ecosystems and public health. The detrimental impact of these non-biodegradable materials is evident in polluted water bodies, compromised soil quality, and the overall degradation of natural habitats. Recognizing the urgency of this environmental crisis, the Green Bag Initiative, orchestrated by KYEG, endeavors to address this issue comprehensively. However, the pressing problem persists as a result of widespread ignorance, inadequate awareness, and the absence of viable alternatives to plastic bags. Consequently, urgent intervention is imperative to reverse the environmental damage, safeguard biodiversity, and pave the way for a sustainable, Plastic-Free Future in Uganda.


Goals

  • Minimize Plastic Pollution: To reduce plastic waste in Uganda by 50% within three years.

  • Promote Sustainable Alternatives: To encourage the adoption of reusable bags and sustainable packaging.

  • Raise Awareness: To educate the community on the detrimental effects of plastic pollution on the environment and public health.

  • Community Engagement: To foster community involvement and commitment to reduce plastic use.

Vision

To create a Uganda that leads the way in environmental conservation where citizens and businesses prioritize eco-friendly practices.

Mission

To reduce plastic pollution and promote sustainable practices in Uganda by engaging the communities, businesses and policymakers through education, advocating for alternative solutions and recycling.
